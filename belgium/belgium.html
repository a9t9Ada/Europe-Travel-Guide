Monuments

Belgium is home to some of Europe’s most captivating landmarks — from the medieval canals of Bruges to the Grand Place in Brussels and the historic belfries scattered across the country. Each monument tells a story, a piece of history that shaped the region. Explore the beauty, culture, and timeless architecture that make Belgium unforgettable.

Fun-Facts

Belgian chocolate isn’t just a treat — it’s practically an institution. Some chocolatiers have been perfecting recipes for over a century.
Waffles in Belgium aren’t just street food; they’re a national obsession, with different regions boasting their own unique styles.
Belgium has three official languages: Dutch, French, and German, depending on the region.
The Atomium in Brussels was originally built for the 1958 World Expo and represents an iron crystal magnified 165 billion times.
Belgium produces around 220,000 tons of chocolate per year, making it one of the world’s top chocolate exporters.
