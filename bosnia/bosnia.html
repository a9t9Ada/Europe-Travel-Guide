Monuments

Bosnia and Herzegovina is a land where history whispers through stone and river. From the timeless arches of the Stari Most in Mostar to the ancient fortresses guarding hilltops, each monument tells a story of resilience, culture, and centuries gone by. Wander through these historic treasures and feel the heartbeat of a nation shaped by time.

Fun-Facts

Sarajevo Tunnel from the 1992–1996 siege isn’t just a historical site—it’s a museum today, showcasing human ingenuity and resilience.
Stari Most in Mostar is so iconic it inspired the bridge in the Marvel movie Captain America: Civil War.
Jajce is the only town in the world with a waterfall in the city center—Pliva River blends naturally with urban life like a fairy tale.
Sarajevo’s fiery past: In 1697, Eugene of Savoy burned Sarajevo to the ground, yet the city rose again from its ashes, rebuilt and thriving.
Vrelo Bosne in Sarajevo is not only the source of the Bosna River but was historically a place for festivals and water games.
