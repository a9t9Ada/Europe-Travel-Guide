Food

Serbian cuisine is celebrated for its rich flavors, hearty ingredients, and comforting dishes. From grilled meats and hearty stews to fresh cheeses and traditional pastries, every meal tells a story of Serbia’s culture and traditions. Discover the tastes shaped by centuries of farming, livestock, and local craftsmanship that continue to delight both locals and travelers alike.

Fun-Facts

Serbia is home to five UNESCO World Heritage sites, showcasing its medieval monasteries and cultural legacy.
Belgrade is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Europe, with a history spanning over 7,000 years.
The Dunav River flows through Serbia and meets the Sava in Belgrade, creating one of the most iconic river confluences in Europe.
Serbia hosts EXIT Festival in Novi Sad, one of Europe’s most famous music festivals, held inside the Petrovaradin Fortress.
The country is known for its hospitality and café culture, where long coffee breaks and socializing are a daily ritual.
Serbia is considered the birthplace of rakija, a traditional fruit brandy deeply rooted in local culture and traditions.
