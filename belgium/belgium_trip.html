Trip Plan 1: Classic Brussels & Landmarks (3 Days)

Day 1 – Brussels Essentials:
  •  Morning: Grand Place – admire the ornate guildhalls and Town Hall, a UNESCO World Heritage site
  •  Afternoon: Manneken Pis – quirky statue and local stories
  •  Evening: Mont des Arts – enjoy panoramic views, fountains, and gardens
Day 2 – Art, History & Museums:
  •  Morning: Atomium – explore the iconic structure and futuristic exhibits
  •  Afternoon: Royal Palace & Magritte Museum – see Belgian royalty’s residence and surrealist art
  •  Evening: Cathedral of St. Michael and St. Gudula – admire Gothic architecture and stained glass
Day 3 – Day Trips & Local Life:
  •  Morning: Mini-Europe or Belgian Comic Strip Center – fun attractions for culture and photography
  •  Afternoon: Delirium Café & local squares – taste Belgian beers and enjoy street life
  •  Evening: Dinner at a traditional Belgian restaurant – sample moules-frites or stoofvlees

Trip Plan 2: Northern Belgium & Historical Cities (4 Days)

Day 1 – Bruges Arrival:
  •  Morning: Explore Bruges canals and medieval streets
  •  Afternoon: Belfry of Bruges – climb for panoramic city views
  •  Evening: Canal-side dinner and stroll around Markt Square
Day 2 – Ghent & Castles:
  •  Morning: Gravensteen – visit the Castle of the Counts and learn about medieval history
  •  Afternoon: St. Bavo’s Cathedral – see the famous Ghent Altarpiece
  •  Evening: Wander along Graslei & Korenlei – enjoy riverside cafes and architecture
Day 3 – Antwerp & Local Culture:
  •  Morning: Cathedral of Our Lady – admire works by Rubens
  •  Afternoon: Antwerp Zoo or MAS Museum – a mix of culture and fun
  •  Evening: Walk through Grote Markt and enjoy Belgian cuisine
Day 4 – Coastal & Countryside Escape:
  •  Morning: Visit Ostend or Knokke-Heist – enjoy Belgian beaches
  •  Afternoon: Try local seafood and Belgian fries by the coast
  •  Evening: Return to Brussels – evening walk and waffles for dessert

Trip Plan 3: Belgian Food & Culture Tour (3 Days)

Day 1 – Brussels Culinary Experience:  Morning: Belgian breakfast – fresh bread, croissants, and hot chocolate  Afternoon: Visit local markets – try Belgian cheeses, chocolates, and pastries  Evening: Dinner at a traditional Belgian bistro – moules-frites or stoofvlees
Day 2 – Iconic Belgian Treats & Cafés:  Morning: Chocolate tasting – visit famous chocolatiers for pralines and truffles  Afternoon: Waffle tasting tour – classic Brussels and Liège waffles  Evening: Walk around Grand Place – enjoy the lively atmosphere and street performers
Day 3 – Sweet & Savory Finale:  Morning: Cooking class – learn to make Belgian specialties like waterzooi or speculoos  Afternoon: Street food tasting – fries, croquettes, and local beers (or non-alcoholic options)  Evening: Sunset at Mont des Arts or along the canal in Bruges & farewell dinner
