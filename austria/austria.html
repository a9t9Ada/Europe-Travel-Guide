Monuments

Austria is a country of majestic landscapes, imperial history, and rich cultural heritage. From the grandeur of Schönbrunn Palace in Vienna to the serene beauty of Hallstatt’s alpine village, each landmark tells a story of Austria’s past and present. Explore the classical music legacy of Salzburg, the artistic treasures of Vienna, and the picturesque charm of Tyrol’s mountains — Austria offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and breathtaking scenery.

Food

Austrian cuisine is renowned for its hearty flavors, rich pastries, and time-honored traditions. From tender Wiener Schnitzel to savory Tafelspitz, from sweet Sachertorte to delicate Apfelstrudel, each dish tells a story of Austria’s culinary heritage. Discover the tastes that shaped the nation’s history and continue to delight locals and visitors alike.

Fun-Facts

Austria is famous for its classical music heritage, being the birthplace of legends like Mozart, Haydn, and Schubert.
Vienna’s historic city center is a UNESCO World Heritage site, recognized for its stunning baroque architecture and cultural significance.
The Viennese coffeehouse culture is so iconic that it’s listed as Intangible Cultural Heritage by UNESCO, where people come to relax, read, and socialize.
Austria produces over 30,000 tonnes of Mozartkugeln (chocolate pralines) every year, honoring the legendary composer.
The country is home to some of the world’s most famous ski resorts in the Alps, making it a winter sports paradise.
The Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra is one of the most prestigious orchestras globally, with concerts like the New Year’s Concert being broadcast worldwide.
