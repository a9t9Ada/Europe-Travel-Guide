Monuments

Estonia is a land of medieval charm, serene landscapes, and captivating architecture. From the cobblestone streets of Tallinn’s Old Town to the historic castles of Tartu and the scenic beauty of Lahemaa National Park, each site tells a story of the nation’s rich heritage. Discover the colors, traditions, and iconic landmarks that make Estonia an unforgettable destination.

Food

Estonian cuisine is celebrated for its hearty flavors, fresh local ingredients, and seasonal traditions. From smoked fish and rye bread to savory stews and wild berry desserts, every dish tells a story of Estonia’s culture and history. Discover the tastes shaped by forests, lakes, and the Baltic Sea that continue to delight locals and travelers alike.

Fun-Facts

Estonia is home to one of the best-preserved medieval old towns in Europe, with Tallinn Old Town being a UNESCO World Heritage site.
Estonian is one of the few Finnic languages in the world, closely related to Finnish but completely different from neighboring Scandinavian languages.
Laulupidu, the Estonian Song Festival, gathers tens of thousands of people every five years to sing together, celebrating national culture and unity.
The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral in Tallinn, built in the Russian Revival style, is one of the most iconic landmarks in the country.
Estonia has over 2,000 islands, many of which are sparsely populated, offering serene landscapes and untouched nature.
Traditional black rye bread and smoked fish are staples of Estonian cuisine, reflecting the country’s connection to the Baltic Sea and agrarian heritage.
