Finland is a land of serene lakes, snowy forests, and magical northern lights. From Helsinki’s charming streets to Lapland’s frozen landscapes, every scene blends nature, tradition, and timeless beauty.

Fun-Facts

Land of a thousand lakes – Finland has over 188,000 lakes, giving it one of the most water-rich landscapes in the world.
Midnight Sun – in the summer, the sun doesn’t set above the Arctic Circle, creating magical 24-hour daylight.
Aurora Borealis – Lapland is one of the best places on Earth to see the Northern Lights dancing across the sky.

Monuments

Finland is a land where history is etched in stone, wood, and tranquil town squares. From medieval castles and fortress walls to grand churches, wooden villages, and monuments honoring its Viking and Swedish past, each site tells tales of kings, sailors, and centuries gone by. Explore these timeless landmarks and discover the serene, poetic beauty that shapes Finland’s heritage.

Fun-Facts

Sauna culture – there are over 2 million saunas in Finland, almost one per household, central to daily life.
Santa Claus Village – Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus, attracting visitors from around the globe.
Design hub – Finland is famous for minimalist design and architecture, with Helsinki recognized as a UNESCO City of Design.

Food

Finnish cuisine is a celebration of nature’s purity, seasonality, and rustic charm. From freshly caught fish and forest berries to hearty rye breads and creamy cheeses, every dish tells a story of snowy forests, serene lakes, and cozy hearths. Discover flavors shaped by centuries of foraging, fishing, and local traditions, inviting you to taste Finland’s wilderness, warmth, and timeless simplicity in every bite.
