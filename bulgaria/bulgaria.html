Fun-Facts

Bulgaria is home to over 9,000 historical monuments, including ancient Thracian tombs, medieval fortresses, and UNESCO World Heritage sites.
Bulgarian is one of the oldest written Slavic languages, with its Cyrillic alphabet still in use today across many countries.
The Rose Valley produces over 50% of the world’s rose oil, making Bulgaria a global leader in perfumery ingredients.
The Rila Monastery, founded in the 10th century, is not only a spiritual center but also a masterpiece of Bulgarian Renaissance architecture.
Bulgaria hosts the International Rose Festival in Kazanlak every year, celebrating its centuries-old tradition of rose cultivation.
Traditional Bulgarian yogurt is famed worldwide for its unique Lactobacillus bulgaricus bacteria, giving it a distinct taste and health benefits.
