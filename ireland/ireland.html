Fun-Facts

Ireland is famous for its lush landscapes, historic castles, and rich cultural traditions.
Irish people celebrate St. Patrick’s Day worldwide, turning cities green in honor of their patron saint.
Ireland is home to over 30,000 castles, ranging from medieval fortresses to stately homes.
The Cliffs of Moher rise over 200 meters above the Atlantic Ocean, offering one of Europe’s most breathtaking coastal views.
Irish is still taught in schools and is an official language alongside English, keeping the ancient Gaelic culture alive.
Ireland is also known for producing some of the world’s finest whiskeys and stouts, including Guinness, which has been brewed in Dublin since 1759.
