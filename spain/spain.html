Fun-Facts

Spain is home to one of the largest numbers of UNESCO World Heritage sites in the world, with over 45 locations celebrating its rich history, architecture, and cultural diversity.
Spanish is the second most spoken native language in the world, making Spain’s influence truly global.
La Tomatina in Buñol is one of the world’s most famous festivals, where thousands of people gather every year for a massive tomato fight.
The Sagrada Família in Barcelona has been under construction for over a century and is still not finished, making it one of the longest-running architectural projects in history.
Paella originated in Valencia and was traditionally cooked outdoors over an open fire, bringing families and communities together.
